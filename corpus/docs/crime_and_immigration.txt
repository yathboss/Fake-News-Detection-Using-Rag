Title: Immigration And Crime Context
Source: Policy and criminology research summary

Research on immigration and crime in the United States often finds that immigrants are not more likely than native-born citizens to commit crimes. Some studies report lower incarceration or offending rates among immigrant groups.

Crime trends vary by location, time period, and offense type. Broad statements claiming that immigrants are uniformly responsible for crime waves are usually not supported by the full body of evidence.

Analysts caution that isolated incidents should not be generalized to entire populations without strong data.
