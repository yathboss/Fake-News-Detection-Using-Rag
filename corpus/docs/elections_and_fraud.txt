Title: Election Fraud Evidence Overview
Source: Election administration and court summary

Reviews of United States elections have found isolated cases of fraud but no evidence of fraud on a scale large enough to overturn national results in recent federal elections. Courts and election officials have repeatedly required evidence-based claims.

Sweeping statements that an election was stolen through massive fraud generally require strong proof. Unsupported allegations, social media posts, or anecdotal reports are not enough by themselves.
