Title: Vaccines And Autism Research Summary
Source: CDC and WHO style summary

Large epidemiological studies have found no credible evidence that routine childhood vaccines cause autism. Public health agencies state that vaccines are tested for safety and continue to be monitored after approval.

The original paper that claimed a link between the MMR vaccine and autism was retracted, and later investigations identified serious problems with that work. Reviews involving large populations have not confirmed a causal connection.

Medical experts note that vaccine side effects can occur, but the evidence does not support the claim that vaccines broadly cause autism.
