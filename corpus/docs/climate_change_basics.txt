Title: Climate Change Scientific Consensus
Source: NASA, IPCC, NOAA summaries

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns, and the current warming trend is primarily driven by human activities, especially the burning of fossil fuels. Carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases trap heat in the atmosphere.

Scientific organizations including NASA, NOAA, and the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change report that global temperatures have risen significantly since the late nineteenth century. Multiple independent measurements show warming in the air, oceans, and land.

Researchers do not describe climate change as a hoax. They cite observed measurements, published studies, and long-term records as evidence of human-caused warming.
